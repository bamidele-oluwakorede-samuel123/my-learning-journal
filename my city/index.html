01 — About

Africa's
Megacity

Lagos is Nigeria's largest city and the economic capital of Africa's most populous nation. Sitting on the Atlantic coast, it is a sprawling metropolis of contrasts — gleaming skyscrapers beside colourful markets, Afrobeats echoing through traffic-jammed streets, and a creative energy that is reshaping the entire continent.

From the bustle of Lagos Island to the beaches of Victoria Island, from the artistry of Yaba to the commerce of Apapa — Lagos is not just a city. It is a force of nature.

02 — Highlights

Must-See
Lagos

🌊

Tarkwa Bay Beach

A hidden lagoon beach accessible only by boat — Lagos's most serene escape from the city noise.

🎨

Nike Art Gallery

Four floors of Nigerian art, textiles, and culture in the heart of Lekki.

🏛️

National Museum

Home to ancient Benin bronzes and artefacts tracing Nigeria's rich civilisations.

🛍️

Balogun Market

The beating commercial heart of Lagos Island — a sensory overload of colour, sound, and trade.

🌉

Third Mainland Bridge

At 11.8km, one of the longest bridges in Africa — connecting the mainland to the island across Lagos Lagoon with breathtaking views at sunrise.

03 — Culture

Where
Culture
is Born

🎵

Afrobeats

Lagos gave the world Afrobeats. From Fela Kuti's Shrine to Burna Boy's sold-out arenas — music runs through Lagos's veins like blood.

🍛

Food & Flavour

Jollof rice debates, suya smoke at midnight, fresh pepper soup by the lagoon — Lagos food is an experience, not just a meal.

👗

Fashion Capital

Lagos Fashion Week draws global attention. Ankara prints, aso-ebi styles, and a new generation of designers putting Lagos on the world map.

🎬

Nollywood

The world's second-largest film industry by volume. Lagos is the creative engine behind thousands of films watched across Africa and beyond.
